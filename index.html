THE HOMEWORK TUBES STORY

From one tube in a garage to 2.4 miles of tubes across the world.

It all started in 1964 on Blossom Hill Road in Los Gatos, California, when retired industrial plumber and amateur dog psychiatrist Sam Grant purchased a vacant fruit packing warehouse and had what he later called "the only good idea I ever had."

Sam had noticed that his own kids did their homework faster when they were inside the large drainage pipes he stored in his yard. He couldn't explain it. Neither could his wife. Their pediatrician said, "I don't think that's true." That was good enough for Sam.

He installed 340 feet of interconnected polyethylene tubes inside the warehouse, placed small desks and battery-powered lamps at various junctions, and opened the doors to the neighborhood. He charged 25 cents per child. Within a week, he had a line around the block. Within two weeks, a second tube had to be added after a child got wedged at a junction for forty-five minutes during peak homework hours. The incident — later known internally as "The First Wedging" — led to the adoption of wider-gauge tubes, which remain the Homework Tubes standard to this day.

By 1978, there were three locations in the Bay Area. By 1992, Homework Tubes had gone national, expanding to twelve states and introducing the Tube Tutor™ intercom system. The original Los Gatos warehouse on Blossom Hill Road is still open — though the tubes are much nicer now, and they've replaced the battery-powered lamps with actual electricity.

TUBE SAFETY

Your child's safety is our #1 priority.

⛑️

Complimentary Tube Helmets

All children are offered a certified Tube Helmet™ upon entry to prevent concussions caused by going headfirst into another child at a tube junction. Helmets are also recommended for the trap door sections, where an unexpected drop onto the cement staging area below has been documented on more than one occasion.

🪟

25% Less Glass

Following a comprehensive 2023 tube audit, we are proud to announce that our tubes now contain 25% less glass than they did in 2022. This is an ongoing effort and we hope to continue reducing glass content year over year. We appreciate your patience.

💪

The Broken Glass Tube

The exception to the above. Introduced in 2024, the Broken Glass Tube is a new addition designed for kids who, in the words of the suggestion box entry that inspired it, "need to learn a little bit of toughness." Long pants and closed-toed shoes are required. Waivers are available at the front desk.

🦺

The Buddy System

After The First Wedging of 1964 and several subsequent wedgings throughout the '70s and '80s, all children under 7 are now required to enter the tubes with a designated Tube Buddy. Tube Buddies are assigned at random. Many lasting friendships have been formed this way. Some rivalries, too.

Homework Tubes™ is not responsible for scrapes, bumps, bruises, disorientation, temporary hearing loss from whistles, or emotional distress caused by the Broken Glass Tube. All children tube at their own risk.

⚠️ Important Notices Regarding Tubey

Homework Assistance Policy: As of March 2023, Tubey is no longer permitted to complete, assist with, or provide answers to children's homework assignments. This policy was implemented after an internal review revealed that Tubey (specifically, the performer inside the Tubey suit at the Schaumburg, IL location) had been doing kids' math homework for them for approximately four months. Seventeen report cards were affected. We apologize to the parents and to the Schaumburg Unified School District.

Catchphrase Discontinuation: Tubey's signature catchphrase, "Let's get tubular!" has been permanently retired effective June 2024. This decision was made on the advice of legal counsel following an incident at the Paramus, NJ location in which a Tubey performer used the phrase while forcefully shoving an adult man back into a tube the man was trying to exit. The man, who had entered the tubes by mistake, filed a civil complaint. The matter was settled out of court for an undisclosed amount. Tubey no longer speaks at events and communicates exclusively through enthusiastic waving.

All Tubey performers now undergo a 40-hour de-escalation training program.
